Notre Dame Center for Research Computing(CRC)

Student Research Intern

I'm a research assistant for the CRC's Triggers of Mass Killings(ToMK) project, which uses natural language processing, data science, and machine learning to identify triggers of state-led mass killings. As part of my work, I've downloaded and cleaned over 600,000 news articles using a Python API for the Lexis-Nexis database. Currently, I'm working on using knowledge graphs in Python to improve text classification.

June-July 2021
NSF Computational Social Science REU

Research Fellow

I continued my work on ToMK project during Summer 2021. I raised text classification accuracy by over 10% by tuning hyperparamters for our three machine learning tools, SVM, BERT, and Longformer. I reduced potential positive articles by 98% by identifying overlap between machine learning tools. At the end of the program, I created a poster summarizing my findings and presented to other researchers at Notre Dame's Summer Research Symposium.
